About

Simplicity is the
ultimate sophistication.

I'm a final-year Robotics Intelligence student at Universitat Jaume I, currently on a research internship at the V4R (Vision for Robotics) lab at TU Wien in Vienna, supervised by Tessa Pulli under Prof. Markus Vincze.

My work focuses on adapting Vision-Language-Action models to the Toyota HSR robot, bridging the gap between large-scale pretrained models and real-world robotic embodiments with non-standard action spaces.

Before Vienna, I co-founded the UJI Robotics Team, led technology at Adagio (an AI accessibility startup), and built systems ranging from ocean-cleaning robots to drone-based 3D reconstruction pipelines. I believe the best engineering feels effortless: simple on the surface, deeply considered underneath.

Current Research · TU Wien
Vision-Language-Action Models for the Toyota HSR

Adapting VLA models (SmolVLA, OpenVLA) to a robot with an 11-dimensional joint-space action space. Comparative study on embodiment mismatch, fine-tuning with the AIRoA MoMa dataset (25K+ episodes), targeting ICRA 2026 workshop.

Drone 3D Reconstruction with Gaussian Splatting

End-to-end pipeline: simulated drone captures multi-altitude orbital imagery, bypasses SfM with ground-truth poses, and trains a 3DGS model. Single-command automation via ROS2 + Gazebo + ArduPilot.
